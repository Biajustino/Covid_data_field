Covid overview

This project has the goal to observe the patterns of the covid pandemic in the year of 2019.

To start this analysis we download a data set from John Hopkins found on this website: https://github.com/CSSEGISandData/COVID-19/tree/master/csse_covid_19_data/csse_covid_19_time_series Then we cleaned the set to facilitate the analyses. We remove columns that wouldnt be used for analyses and also rows with negative values for cases since you cant have negative covid cases. Finally, we end up with 2 main datasets: Global(all cases and deaths), US(all US cases and deaths).

It is possible to point out how the graphics have waves that intensify during the years, especially in 2022. A couple of new variants of the disease can explain that. We can also observe that both the Global data and the US data have their peaks of death in 2021, having some waves after that, but never reaching the same level. The Covid vaccine was launched at the end 2020 and spread along in 2021, so that could be an explanation.

The second part of our analysis was to observe the number of cases and deaths in each state of the US. To keep our analyses clean, we created a data set group by each state and only observed the states with more than the mean in each statistic.

It was possible to observe that Texas and California had the highest overall number of cases in the country. This was, somehow, expected considering that they are the second and third biggest states in the US. However, when observing the number of deaths, New York is at the same level as both states.

Modeling

Another interesting analysis would be to relate the number of cases with the number of deaths and observe if they grow in a linear patter. However, the projection was way off, showing that there isnt a direct relationship between these 2 sets of data.

Bias

When talking about Covid, it is important to keep in mind that this data is self-reported, therefore, the number of cases is based on people who first took the test. Secondly, they informed authorities that they tested positive. With the at-home tests, it became more and more difficult to be sure if the numbers are accurate. Another important fact to keep in mind is that it is hard to guarantee that someone died of covid or from complications from other diseases. For example, a person who has asthma for their lifetime and dies after contracting Covid is a victim of the illness, but maybe would survive under different conditions. Without a clear profile of the victims, it is hard guarantee that the data is not biased.

Conclusions

The Covid pandemic changed the world and understanding the data surrounding this phenomenon is crucial. A couple of observations could be made from the data. First, the number of new cases have come in waves. Secondly, the fact that the number of new deaths reduced significantly since the vaccination effort started is a good indication that the vaccines are working. We can also say that the states with the most cases are, for the most part, the ones with the most deaths. Lastly, it is possible to conclude that cases and deaths dont have a linear correlation.
